    How to make sushi

    Below are basic instructions on making sushi.

    After that there are specific instructions and ingredients for specific rolls.

    Necessary Tools

    Rice cooker:

    Electric Rice Cookers can make making rice a lot more convenient. Preferable use Japanese sushi rice. Go to your local Asian market if you can't find it.

    Rice paddle:

    After the rice is cooked you need to mix in rice vinegar into the rice. A paddle helps disperse the vinegar easily.

    Bowl of Water:

    Your hands will get sticky from working and placing rice onto the mat. So wash your hands often.

    Rolling Mat:

    Cover your mat in plastic wrap to keep rice from sticking to your mat. The mat is used to roll and shape your sushi.

    Sharp Knife:

    Remember to make clean cuts. Make sure your knife isn't dull otherwise it will squash your rolls. Wrapping the roll before cutting can help keep roll together but is optional.If you are cutting your ingredients remember to slice into strips approximately the diameter of a dime to make roll assembly easier.

    Perfect Rice for 3 rolls

    1 Cup

    Rice

    1.2 Cups

    Water

    2.5 Tablespoons

    Rice Vinegar

    0.5 Tablespoons

    Sugar

    0.5 Tablespoon

    Salt

    Remember to add the vinegar afterwards.

    Scale numbers to however amount of rolls you want to make.

    Assembly Instructions

  • 1) Place down nori onto your mat.
  • 2) On one side spread rice in an even layer.
  • 3) Flip over and assemble your roll inner ingredients. Make sure to line them up so each roll has the same stuff when cut.
  • 4) Roll the mat from the buttom going upwards. Here you can decide if you want your roll to be squared or circular by adjusting how you press on the roll with the mat.
  • 5) If your roll has ingredients on the outside other than Rice you can layer them on evenly onto the made roll on top. Optionally cover assembled roll in plastic wrap to make cutting easier (recommended if you put ingredients on outside).
  • 6) Cut your roll into 8 even segments. Easiest way to do this is first cut in half, then cut the two halves in half, and repeat one more time for the two halves.
  • 7) Ready to serve.
